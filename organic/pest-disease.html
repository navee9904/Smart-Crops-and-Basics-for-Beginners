Prevention First

Healthy plants resist pests and disease better.

Well-fed plants have stronger immune systems. Build soil organic matter and avoid synthetic fertilizers that create weak, sappy growth.

Monocultures attract pests. Mix plant families, add flowers for beneficials, and rotate crops annually.

üíß Proper Watering

Overhead watering spreads disease. Water at soil level in morning. Stressed plants (too wet or too dry) attract problems.

Ladybugs, lacewings, and parasitic wasps hunt pests. Plant dill, fennel, and alyssum to attract them.

üßÑ Homemade Sprays

Garlic spray repels soft-bodied insects. Soap spray kills aphids on contact. Neem oil works on many pests.

Row covers block flying pests. Copper tape stops slugs. Sticky traps monitor and reduce populations.
