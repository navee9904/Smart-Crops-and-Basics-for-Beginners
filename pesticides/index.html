Integrated Pest Management

Modern pest control is about more than just spraying chemicals. Integrated Pest Management (IPM) combines biological, cultural, and chemical methods to control pests sustainably.

The goal is not to eliminate all pests, but to keep them below damaging levels while preserving beneficial insects and the ecosystem.

Common Crop Pests

Identify these threats before they damage your yield.

Aphids

Tiny sap-sucking insects that cluster on new growth. They spread viruses and weaken plants. Ladybugs are natural predators.

Caterpillars

Larvae of moths and butterflies that chew leaves. Handpicking works for small infestations. Bt (Bacillus thuringiensis) is an organic option.

Whiteflies

Small white-winged insects that cause yellowing. Sticky traps help monitor populations. Neem oil is effective for control.

Responsible Use

Always follow label instructions. Spray during calm weather, avoid blooming periods to protect pollinators, and never exceed recommended dosages.
