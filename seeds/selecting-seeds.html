Why Seed Quality Matters

The quality of seeds directly determines germination success, plant vigor, and yield. Poor seeds waste time, money, and effort.

Four key criteria: purity, germination rate, moisture content, and disease-free status.

Quality Factors

Purity (98%+)

Proportion of true crop seeds vs contaminants (weed seeds, debris). High purity ensures you grow what you intend.

Germination Rate (85%+)

Percentage of seeds that will sprout. Low rates mean planting more seeds for the same number of plants.

Moisture Content (8-12%)

Seeds must be properly dried for storage. Excess moisture causes fungal growth and premature germination.

Disease-Free

Certified seeds are tested and treated to eliminate seed-borne pathogens. Prevents crop losses from the start.

Certified vs Saved Seeds

Factor Certified Seeds Saved Seeds
Quality Guaranteed standards Variable, can decline
Cost Higher upfront Free (from harvest)
Disease Risk Tested & treated May accumulate pathogens

⚠️ Avoid Unreliable Sources

Seeds from unverified vendors may have low germination, poor purity, or carry diseases. Always buy from reputable suppliers with certification marks.
