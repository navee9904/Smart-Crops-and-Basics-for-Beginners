The Science of Plant Nutrition

Plants require essential nutrients to grow, flower, and produce fruit. Understanding the role of Nitrogen (N), Phosphorus (P), and Potassium (K) is the foundation of effective fertilization.

Over-fertilizing wastes money and harms the environment. Under-fertilizing limits your yield. Learn to find the perfect balance.

NPK Basics Micronutrients Application

Primary Nutrients Explained

Every fertilizer bag displays an NPK ratio. Here's what it means.

Nitrogen (N)

Promotes leafy, green growth. Essential for vegetables and lawns. Deficiency causes yellowing leaves.

Phosphorus (P)

Supports root development and flowering. Critical during early growth and fruit set.

Potassium (K)

Strengthens overall plant health and disease resistance. Improves fruit quality and drought tolerance.

Beginner Tip

Start with a balanced fertilizer (like 10-10-10) for general use. As you learn more about your specific crops and soil, you can adjust to specialized formulas.
