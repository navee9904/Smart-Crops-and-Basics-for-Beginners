Understanding NPK

Every fertilizer bag displays an NPK ratio (e.g., 10-10-10). These numbers represent the percentage of Nitrogen, Phosphorus, and Potassium.

Each nutrient plays a distinct role in plant development. Understanding this helps you choose the right fertilizer for each growth stage.

The Big Three

Nitrogen (N)

Function: Promotes leafy, green growth. Essential for photosynthesis.

Deficiency Signs: Yellowing leaves (especially older ones), stunted growth.

Best For: Leafy vegetables, lawns, early growth stages.

Phosphorus (P)

Function: Supports root development, flowering, and fruit set.

Deficiency Signs: Purple-tinged leaves, poor root growth, delayed flowering.

Best For: Transplants, fruiting crops, early establishment.

Potassium (K)

Function: Strengthens overall plant health, disease resistance, and drought tolerance.

Deficiency Signs: Brown leaf edges, weak stems, poor fruit quality.

Best For: Fruit quality, stress tolerance, mature plants.

Common NPK Ratios

Ratio Use Case
10-10-10 Balanced, general purpose
20-10-10 Lawns, leafy vegetables
5-10-10 Root crops, flowering plants
0-10-10 Fruit development, late season

Start Balanced

New to fertilizing? A balanced 10-10-10 works for most situations. As you learn your crops' needs, adjust the ratio accordingly.
