The Art of Fertilizer Application

Knowing what to apply is just half the battle—how and when you apply it determines whether your plants actually benefit.

Proper application ensures nutrients reach the root zone efficiently, minimizes waste, and protects the environment from runoff.

Application Methods

Broadcasting

Spreading fertilizer evenly over the soil surface. Good for lawns and pre-planting preparation. Work into soil for best results.

Side-dressing

Applying fertilizer in bands alongside growing plants. Ideal for mid-season nitrogen boost without disturbing roots.

Foliar Feeding

Spraying diluted fertilizer on leaves. Quick uptake for micronutrient deficiencies. Apply early morning or evening.

Fertigation

Delivering fertilizer through irrigation water. Provides consistent, efficient feeding. Common in drip systems.

Timing Guide

Growth Stage Focus Recommended
Transplanting Root establishment Phosphorus-rich (e.g., 5-10-5)
Vegetative Leaf & stem growth Nitrogen-rich (e.g., 20-10-10)
Flowering Blooms & fruit set Balanced (e.g., 10-10-10)
Fruiting Fruit development Potassium-rich (e.g., 5-10-15)

⚠️ Avoid Over-fertilizing

More is not better. Excess fertilizer burns roots, pollutes water, and wastes money. Always follow label rates and test soil before major applications.
