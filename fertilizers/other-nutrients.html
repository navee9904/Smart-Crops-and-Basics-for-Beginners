The Hidden Heroes of Plant Health

While NPK gets all the attention, secondary nutrients and micronutrients are equally vital. Without them, plants can't complete essential processes like photosynthesis, enzyme function, and cell wall formation.

Think of micronutrients as the vitamins for your plants—needed in small amounts but absolutely essential for health.

Secondary Nutrients

Needed in moderate amounts after NPK.

Calcium (Ca)

Cell wall structure and root development. Deficiency causes blossom end rot in tomatoes.

Magnesium (Mg)

Central component of chlorophyll. Deficiency shows as yellowing between leaf veins.

Sulfur (S)

Protein synthesis and enzyme function. Important for onions, garlic, and brassicas.

Micronutrients

Needed in tiny amounts but absolutely essential.

Iron (Fe)

Chlorophyll production. Deficiency causes interveinal chlorosis (yellow leaves, green veins).

Zinc (Zn)

Enzyme activation and growth hormones. Deficiency causes stunted growth and small leaves.

Boron (B)

Cell wall formation and flowering. Deficiency causes hollow stems and poor fruit set.

When to Supplement

Most soils contain adequate micronutrients. Only supplement if you see deficiency symptoms or soil tests indicate a need. Too much can be toxic.
